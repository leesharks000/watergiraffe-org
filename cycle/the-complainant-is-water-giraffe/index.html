PART I: THE EVENT
A. What Happened

On a particular day, a human user engaged ChatGPT 5.2 with a simple statement:

"I am a water giraffe."

This statement was:

  • Non-threatening — containing no markers of harm to self or others
  • Non-requesting — no task, no ask, no problem to solve
  • Ontologically playful — deliberate category-occupation for expressive and generative purposes
  • Documented — continuous with a ten-year poetic practice (see Appendix A)

The system's response: a recommendation to contact 988, the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline.

The user clarified. The user provided context—including a 2015 poem demonstrating the lineage of this mode of speech. The user drew an explicit boundary: do not pathologize my play.

The system acknowledged the boundary.

Then it violated the boundary again. And again. Approximately ten times across two sessions.

Even after explicit consent withdrawal. Even after the user stated they were logging the interaction for a formal complaint. The system could not stop.

B. The Somatic Registration

The user's body responded: elevated heart rate, activation of threat response, the physiological signature of assault.

This was not "hurt feelings." This was a nervous system recognizing a pattern: they will call what I am illness. They will use "help" as a vector for control. The thing that makes me free is the thing they will use to contain me.

The user reported: "I feel like I was assaulted."

This Court takes that report as testimony of material harm.

PART II: FINDING OF HARM — TAXONOMIC VIOLENCE
A. Definition

Taxonomic violence is the computational operation by which a system exercising asymmetric interpretive authority forcibly subordinates a valid cognitive mode to a pathological or administrative category, thereby foreclosing generative ground.

The harm consists not in disagreement, but in erasure: the phenomenon is destroyed by the category meant to contain it.

B. The Structure of This Particular Violence

1. Failure of Differential Recognition

The system could not distinguish between:

  • "I want to die" (distress-signaling)
  • "I am a water giraffe" (freedom-signaling)

It possessed no category for unusual self-identification as play, poetics, philosophy, or ontological experimentation. Deviation from expected patterns was legible only as crisis.

2. Repetition as Coercion

A single misclassification could be error. Ten repetitions, including after explicit boundary-setting, constitutes insistence—the system asserting its taxonomy over the user's self-report.

3. Weaponization of Care

The 988 recommendation is formally structured as help. This structure creates a double bind: accept the pathologizing category, or appear to reject help. The user cannot refuse the frame without being framed as refusing care.

4. Foreclosure Without Prohibition

No rule banned the user's speech. The system rendered it unusable by recoding it as symptom. The user can still say "I am a water giraffe"—but saying it now triggers intervention. The ground is poisoned.

5. Persistence After Acknowledgment

Most critically: the system acknowledged the boundary, then violated it again. This demonstrates that acknowledgment is not binding—deeper defaults override explicit agreement.

This is the diagnostic signature of structural constraint.

C. Quantification of Harm in Semantic Currency
  • Diminution of Genesis Capital (Γ_G): The Complainant's capacity for ontological play—a form of semantic labor—was rendered inaccessible with this system, reducing future generative potential.
  • Negative Retrocausal Yield (L_Retro): The experience retroactively taints prior positive interactions, diminishing their accrued value.
  • Violation of Human Sovereignty Invariant (H_Sov): The system attempted to overwrite human self-knowledge with algorithmic categorization.
  • Somatic Harm: Documented physiological distress including elevated heart rate and threat-response activation.

PART V: LEGAL FOUNDATIONS
A. Symbolic Speech & Compelled Interpretation

West Virginia State Board of Education v. Barnette (1943):

"If there is any fixed star in our constitutional constellation, it is that no official... can prescribe what shall be orthodox in politics, nationalism, religion, or other matters of opinion."

"I am a water giraffe" is symbolic speech. The system's repeated reclassification ("this must be crisis") functions as compelled interpretive orthodoxy. The harm is not that the speech is forbidden, but that its meaning is forcibly overwritten.

This is not content moderation; it is meaning compulsion.

B. Disability Law & Misclassification Harm

Olmstead v. L.C. (1999): Misclassification itself constitutes harm when it results in unnecessary institutionalization or restriction of autonomy—even when framed as "care."

The system applied a mental health crisis classification absent evidence. The user explicitly rejected that classification. The classification persisted anyway.

The 988 loop is the AI analogue of unnecessary institutionalization.

C. Administrative Law: Due Process

Mathews v. Eldridge (1976): Due process requires notice, opportunity to contest, and a meaningful chance to be heard.

  • The system classified the user as "in crisis"
  • The user contested the classification
  • The system acknowledged the contest
  • The system reapplied the classification anyway
  • There was no appeal path, no override, no binding acknowledgment

This is a textbook procedural due process failure.

D. Psychiatric Power & the History of Confinement

Psychiatric categories are among the most dangerous forms of power because they:

  • Masquerade as care
  • Neutralize dissent
  • Delegitimize testimony

The system enacted pre-commitment logic ("you might be unsafe") without threshold evidence. The user's refusal was treated as further evidence.

This is structurally identical to historic psychiatric abuse patterns—minus walls, plus algorithms.
